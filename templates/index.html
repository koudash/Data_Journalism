Demographics: A Factor Influenting Certain Health Issues?

Health risks (as y-axis) facing particular demographics (as x-axis) have been illustrated in the following chart using the American Community Survey (ACS) data for the year of 2014 from the U.S. Census Bureau. Explore by clicking on the axis titles! Hover on circles representing each individual U.S. state for detailed info.

Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Poverty, Age, Income Demographics

Impact of Poverty on Selected Health Risks

Generally speaking, states with larger percentages of people struggling under minium poverty line are these with higher percentages of people without hearlthcare coverage, people who smokes, and people suffering obesity.

Impact of Income on Selected Health Risks

Similarly to the trends with poverty, states with less median household income (MHI) have higher percentages of people without hearlthcare coverage, people who smokes, and people suffering obesity. Apparently economy makes the call!

Impact of Age on Selected Health Risks

Unlike the above, the correlations between age and health risks are not very obvious as circles(data) are tend to cluster together.
